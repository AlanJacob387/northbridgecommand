The Problem

Hotels operate blind.

A 200-room hotel spends $400,000–$600,000 annually on housekeeping. Without accurate occupancy data, housekeeping is dispatched to occupied rooms, HVAC runs in vacant ones, and late checkouts go undetected until it's too late.

Every hour of uncertainty costs money. None of the existing solutions actually solve the problem.

How It Works

Proprietary presence detection.

Meridian uses proprietary radio frequency sensing technology to determine, with certainty, whether a room is occupied or vacant. The underlying algorithms are our IP. What matters is the result.

01
Install in 15 Minutes
A small set of discreet devices are mounted in the room and connected to wall power. No structural changes. No hotel network access required. No IT department involvement.
02
Continuous Sensing
The system monitors the room continuously and passively. No cameras. No wearables. No interaction with guests. Nothing for guests to notice or object to.
03
Proprietary Detection
Onboard processing runs our detection algorithms locally. No identifiable data is extracted. The system determines one thing: is a human present or not.
04
Occupied / Vacant
A binary status — Occupied or Vacant — is sent to your dashboard. Nothing else leaves the room. The hotel sees only the decision.

Privacy Architecture

The guarantee is physical, not policy.

It is physically impossible to reconstruct an image or identify an individual from these signals. The system determines one thing: is a human present. Nothing more.
Meridian Technical Architecture
01
Range resolution of 7.5 metres
The radio frequency technology used operates at a resolution that cannot form an image. The physics prevent it. No regulatory carve-out needed because there is nothing to regulate.
02
Nothing identifiable stored or transmitted
All processing happens locally inside the room. Only the binary occupancy decision is sent to the dashboard. No signal data, no biometrics, nothing identifiable ever leaves the device.
03
PIPEDA, CCPA, GDPR — categorically exempt
Camera-based systems face increasing legal and reputational exposure across North America and Europe. Meridian is not a surveillance system. It was never designed to be.
